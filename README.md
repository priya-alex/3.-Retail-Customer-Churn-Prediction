3. Retail Customer Churn Prediction
1. Description / Background
Retail businesses face revenue loss due to customer churn. With rising competition in e-commerce and offline stores, retaining loyal customers is more cost-effective than acquiring new ones. Predictive analytics can help identify at-risk customers and guide retention campaigns.
2. Problem Statement
•	No predictive system for churn.
•	Marketing spends are wasted on customers who would stay anyway.
•	High customer attrition reduces revenue and market share.
3. Task
•	Clean customer transaction & demographic data.
•	Build churn prediction model (Yes/No).
•	Identify top churn drivers (low purchase frequency, complaints, discounts).
•	Create a Power BI dashboard with churn rates, customer segments, and retention suggestions.
4. Objective
•	Predict churn with >80% recall.
•	Enable proactive customer retention campaigns.
•	Reduce acquisition costs and improve CLV (Customer Lifetime Value).
5. Scope
In scope: Data cleaning, EDA, classification modeling, customer segmentation, dashboard.
Out of scope: Real-time SMS/email campaign automation.
6. Stakeholders
•	Primary: Marketing team, Customer service.
•	Secondary: Senior management, Finance team.
7. Data Needs
•	Demographics (age, gender, income, location).
•	Purchase history (frequency, recency, monetary value).
•	Complaints & support interactions.
•	Loyalty program data.
8. Metrics
•	Churn Rate (%).
•	CLV uplift after retention campaigns.
•	Precision/Recall/F1 of churn model.
9. Tools
Python (Pandas, Scikit-learn), Power BI.
10. Deliverables
•	customer_churn_scores.csv
•	Predictive model notebooks.
•	Power BI dashboard (churn segments, retention KPIs).
•	Final report with recommendations.
